How the library is shaped

The laboratory is often described as a room, a department, or a menu of tests. In practice, it behaves like a system of measurement and responsibility that produces evidence. That evidence moves. It influences decisions. It creates risk when misread or misplaced.

Hubs act as dashboards. Articles are long-lived explanations. Resources point outward to downloads.

Instruments are not oracles. They are translators with assumptions.
