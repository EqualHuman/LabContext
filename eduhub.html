How to use this library

  • You do not need to read in order.
  • Start with what feels unclear.
  • Use cross-links to build a mental model.
  • Leave, return, and reread without pressure.
Concept boundary: LabContext explains systems. It does not teach procedures, validate competency, or score learning.

How the hub is structured

A concept library organized by department. Each department hub uses the same four lenses so the structure stays consistent across the site.

Modules

Foundations
  • What the department is measuring
  • The baseline system logic that makes results behave the way they do
  • Terms and mental models used throughout the section
Constraints
  • Assumptions that results quietly depend on
  • Common reasons a result can be “correct” but still misleading
  • Where interpretation breaks without context
Patterns
  • Recurring result behaviors that appear in real life
  • Which signals tend to move together (and which don’t)
  • Persistence over snapshots
Interfaces

  • Shared signals across systems
  • Boundaries between pre-analytic, analytic, and reporting logic

A clarification authority

LabContext is interpretive, not evaluative. It exists to make hidden logic visible: why systems behave the way they do, why patterns recur, and why those patterns change when constraints shift. It does not teach procedures, provide validation protocols, or perform competency training.
