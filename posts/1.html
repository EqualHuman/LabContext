How to read a lab result: a practical framework

By LabContext • Jan 5, 2026 • Interpretation

Laboratory values are often presented without the narrative that makes them meaningful. Here is a simple sequence to interpret a lab result:

1. Confirm the question

Why was the test ordered? Screening, diagnosis, monitoring, or reassurance?

2. Check reference ranges and method

Reference ranges vary by lab and method; absolute numbers alone are rarely the whole story.

3. Look for clinical context

Compare with symptoms, other tests, and the pre-test probability.

4. Consider limits & uncertainty

Is the change clinically meaningful? Is it within technical variation?

This post is illustrative — LabContext provides interpretation frameworks, not personalised medical advice.
