How the hub is structured

A reference library grouped by department. Each department uses the same four lenses, so once you learn the structure, every hub feels familiar.

The four lenses

Every department uses the same lenses. This reduces cognitive load—your attention goes to the content, not the navigation.

Foundations

  • What the department is measuring
  • The logic that makes results behave the way they do
  • Terms and mental models used throughout

Constraints

  • Assumptions that results quietly depend on
  • How a result can be “correct” but misleading
  • What context is required to interpret safely

Patterns

  • Common result behaviors seen in real life
  • Which signals move together (and which don’t)
  • Persistence over snapshots

Interfaces

  • Connections that prevent siloed interpretation
  • Shared signals across systems
  • Boundaries between pre-analytic, analytic, reporting

Suggested starting point

If you want a guided example of how the hubs read, start with Lab Orientation.

LabContext
A clarification authority

LabContext is interpretive, not evaluative. It exists to make hidden logic visible: why systems behave the way they do, why patterns recur, and why those patterns change when constraints shift. It does not teach procedures, provide validation protocols, or perform competency training.
